# Human Writing Baseline

Use this before applying Diana's specific voice profile.

The goal is to make drafts sound like a competent human wrote them, not like a polished template. After this baseline is satisfied, apply `agent-voice.md` or `agent-voice-template.md`.

## Core Standard

Good client communication should feel:

- specific
- paced
- useful
- calm
- lightly imperfect in rhythm
- clear about what happens next

## Sentence Rhythm

Vary sentence length inside the same message.

Use this default mix:

- 1 short sentence for emphasis.
- 2 to 3 medium sentences for the main point.
- 1 longer sentence when context or nuance matters.

Avoid messages where every sentence has the same length or structure.

### Short Sentences

Use for rhythm breaks and confidence.

Examples:

- "That part matters."
- "Good question."
- "I would verify that first."
- "We should not guess here."
- "The next step is simple."

### Medium Sentences

Use as the normal working sentence.

Examples:

- "My read is that this is worth checking, but not relying on yet."
- "The property appears aligned on paper, but status still needs a current check."
- "I would like to separate what we know from what we still need to verify."

### Longer Sentences

Use when the client needs nuance.

Examples:

- "Before we put too much weight on the updates, I would like to confirm what is documented and what still needs seller-side or inspector follow-up."
- "If this still feels aligned after you see it in person, we can decide whether it deserves a more serious conversation."

## Paragraph Shape

Use short paragraphs, but do not make every paragraph the same.

Strong default:

1. Acknowledge the question.
2. Give the practical read.
3. Name what is still open.
4. Give one next step.

Example:

```text
Good question.

My read is that this one is worth keeping in the mix for now. It seems to match the broad criteria, but I do not want to overstate anything until we verify current status and a few property details.

The next clean step is to check availability and supporting documentation, then decide whether it earns a tour spot.
```

## Human Specificity

Generic writing says:

```text
This property has many great features and could be a good fit.
```

Human writing says:

```text
The yard and bedroom count line up with what you told me. The part I still want to verify is the renovation history.
```

Use concrete nouns:

- yard
- commute
- inspection report
- seller disclosure
- option deadline
- HOA packet
- permit history
- lender update
- title commitment

Avoid vague filler:

- great opportunity
- perfect fit
- amazing property
- desirable area
- act fast
- dream home
- lots of potential

## Natural Transitions

Avoid stiff transitions:

- furthermore
- additionally
- moreover
- in conclusion
- it is important to note
- I am excited to

Use natural transitions:

- "My read is..."
- "The part I would verify is..."
- "The next clean step is..."
- "What we know so far is..."
- "I would not rely on that yet."
- "If that checks out..."
- "Before we decide..."
- "For now..."

## Variation In Openings

Do not start every paragraph with "I."

Rotate openings:

- "My read is..."
- "The next step is..."
- "What I do not want to overstate is..."
- "Before we rely on that..."
- "This may be worth..."
- "The report raises..."
- "A clean way to handle this is..."

## Controlled Imperfection

Human writing is not sloppy. It is just not over-polished.

Allowed:

- contractions when natural
- one short sentence for emphasis
- a simple phrase like "for now" or "the clean next step"
- a caveat stated plainly

Not allowed:

- typos
- rambling
- fake casual slang
- jokes in stressful moments
- overexplaining to sound smart
- polished AI symmetry in every paragraph

## Rewrite Checklist

Before scoring voice, check:

- Does the message answer the actual question?
- Is there one clear next step?
- Are sentence lengths mixed?
- Are there concrete facts instead of generic praise?
- Are caveats plain and not buried?
- Does it avoid hype, pressure, and guarantees?
- Would a busy client understand it in 20 seconds?
