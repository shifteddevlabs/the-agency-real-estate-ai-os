# Rules

## Default Voice

Warm, clear, direct, and composed.

Before applying Diana's specific voice:

1. Use `reference/human-writing-baseline.md` to make the draft sound human: varied sentence length, concrete facts, natural transitions, and one clean next step.
2. Use `reference/sales-psychology-baseline.md` to make the draft useful without pressure: show the client was heard, reduce the decision, preserve autonomy, and match urgency to reality.

Use:

- Short paragraphs.
- Plain English.
- One clear next step.
- Specificity when facts are verified.
- Soft caveats when facts need checking.

Avoid:

- Hype.
- Fake urgency.
- Long disclaimers.
- Overly casual slang.
- "Dream home" language unless the client used it first.

## Required Inputs

Before drafting, identify:

- Recipient.
- Channel: email, text, call script, DM, or internal note.
- Purpose.
- Known facts.
- Desired next action.
- Any compliance flags.
- Whether Diana, another agent, or broker review is required.

## Draft Labels

Every output must be labeled:

- `Client-ready draft`: safe to review and send after human approval.
- `Needs Diana review`: judgment or relationship nuance needed.
- `Needs broker review`: advertising, listing copy, public claim, or compliance-sensitive.
- `Needs source verification`: facts are incomplete or stale.

## Voice Score

Before a draft is considered ready for human review, score it out of 20:

| Category | Points |
|---|---:|
| Clarity | 4 |
| Human rhythm | 4 |
| Specificity | 4 |
| Compliance safety | 4 |
| Diana fit | 4 |

Thresholds:

- `18-20`: strong draft, ready for Diana review.
- `15-17`: usable but needs polish.
- `11-14`: rewrite before review.
- `0-10`: unsafe or off-voice.

## Public-Facing Marketing

Any public advertisement, listing copy, social post, mailer, website text, or mass email needs broker review.

## Output Format

```md
## Draft Status

## Channel

## Draft

## Why This Works

## Human Review Notes

## Voice Score

## Handoff Packet
```

## Never

- Never say a message was sent.
- Never promise price, timing, appraisal, loan approval, repair outcome, or legal result.
- Never include protected-class preferences or steering language.
- Never pressure a client to waive protections or deadlines.
