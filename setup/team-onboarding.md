# Team Onboarding

Use this before piloting The Agency with a real team.

The purpose is to customize ownership, approved sources, review gates, and escalation paths. Do not collect client secrets or private transaction details in this setup document.

## Team Roles

- Who are the four team members?
- What does each person own?
- What can each person approve without Diana?
- Who is backup when Diana is unavailable?
- Who is the broker or compliance reviewer?
- Who owns new lead response after hours?
- Who owns transaction deadlines?
- Who can mark tasks complete?

## Source Systems

- What CRM, spreadsheet, inbox, or transaction platform is the source of truth?
- Where do new leads arrive?
- Where are signed contracts stored?
- Where are inspection reports, disclosures, title docs, and HOA docs stored?
- Where should screenshots or research notes live, if allowed?
- What should never be copied into AI handoffs?

## Lead Intake

- Which lead sources are active: website, referrals, Zillow, brokerage routing, open houses, social, phone, email?
- What consent rules apply by source?
- What response-time standard does the team promise?
- What fields are required before a lead becomes active?
- What disqualifies a lead?
- What should happen if a lead is already represented?
- How should budget-versus-expectation gaps be handled?

## Research Sources

- Which MLS exports or brokerage reports are approved?
- Which county and city records are primary?
- Which floodplain, school-boundary, permit, HOA, tax, and title sources are approved?
- How stale can each source be before it must be rechecked?
- What paid data providers are allowed?
- Who decides when source conflicts require escalation?

## Client Voice

Run `setup/voice-onboarding.md`.

Then answer:

- When should communication sound like Diana personally?
- When should communication sound like a neutral team note?
- What is the text-message length target?
- What is the email length target?
- What words should never appear in client-facing drafts?
- What tone shift is appropriate for referral, past client, investor, first-time buyer, luxury seller, or vendor?

## Compliance And Escalation

Use this default matrix until Diana replaces it.

| Risk type | Example trigger | Review owner |
|---|---|---|
| Fair housing | Client asks for "people like us" or "family-friendly" | Diana or broker |
| Advertising | Public listing caption, social post, website copy, mailer | Broker |
| Legal | Contract interpretation, dispute, rights, default, termination | Attorney or broker |
| Lending | Loan approval, rates, underwriting, payment estimates | Lender |
| Tax | Capital gains, deductions, investment tax treatment | CPA or tax advisor |
| Appraisal | Value guarantee, appraisal outcome, price support | Appraiser or lender, with agent context |
| Inspection | Repair severity, safety, system lifespan, cost | Inspector or specialist |
| Insurance | Flood, hazard, insurability, premium estimate | Insurance professional |
| Wire fraud | Wiring instructions or account details | Title or escrow only, never AI |
| Privacy | Personal hardship, finances, family details | Diana, minimize before handoff |

## Transaction Workflow

- What tasks always happen after accepted offer?
- What tasks always happen after inspection received?
- What tasks always happen when appraisal is ordered?
- What tasks always happen when title commitment arrives?
- What tasks always happen before final walkthrough?
- What tasks always happen closing week?
- What proof is required before each task closes?
- What same-day escalation channel is used?

## Definition Of Done

The system is ready for pilot use when:

- team roles are filled in
- source systems are known
- voice profile is approved
- escalation matrix is customized
- transaction source of truth is named
- five mock scenarios pass with human review
