# Glossary

Real estate jargon, defined for a non-realtor reader. The Agency references many of these terms in its specialist folders. Defined once here so you don't have to infer.

Texas-specific terms are marked `(TX)`.

## A

**Amendment** — A signed change to an executed contract. Used to add or modify terms after both sides have signed. In Texas, often the TREC 39 form (current version) for renegotiating repairs or credits after inspection.

**Appraisal** — A licensed appraiser's estimate of a property's market value. Required by lenders before closing on a financed deal. If the appraisal comes in below contract price, the buyer must renegotiate, bring cash to cover the gap, or use the financing addendum to terminate.

**Appraisal contingency** — A contract clause letting the buyer terminate or renegotiate if the appraisal comes in low. In Texas, embedded in the Third Party Financing Addendum.

## B

**Broker** — A licensed real estate professional who supervises agents. Diana's broker is the compliance owner of last resort. Many decisions require broker review (advertising, agency questions, unusual negotiations).

**Buyer rep agreement** — A signed written contract between a buyer and a buyer's agent. Required before any home showing as of August 2024 (NAR settlement). In Texas, the most common form is TXR-1501.

**Buyer's agent** — A real estate agent representing the buyer in a transaction. Different from the listing agent (who represents the seller).

## C

**CD (Closing Disclosure)** — A federally required document the lender provides the buyer at least 3 business days before closing. Itemizes every cost, loan term, and final number. If anything material changes, the 3-day clock restarts and closing delays.

**Closing** — The final step of a transaction. Buyer and seller sign final documents, money changes hands, the deed records, and possession transfers.

**Closing costs** — Fees paid at closing in addition to the purchase price: title insurance, lender fees, escrow, recording fees, prepaid taxes and insurance, etc. Typically 2-5% of purchase price for the buyer.

**CMA (Comparative Market Analysis)** — A report comparing the subject property to recently sold and currently listed similar properties. Used to suggest a price range, not a single number.

**Commission** — The fee paid to real estate agents at closing. Historically the seller paid both sides; post-NAR 2024, buyer commission is negotiated directly with the buyer.

**Comp / Comparable** — A recently sold property similar to the subject in size, location, and condition. The basis of pricing analysis.

**Contingency** — A condition that must be satisfied before the contract is binding. Common contingencies: financing, appraisal, inspection, sale of buyer's current home.

**Contract** — The signed agreement between buyer and seller. In Texas, residential resale typically uses TREC 20-17 (or current version) plus relevant addenda.

## D

**Deed** — The legal document transferring ownership of real property. Recorded at the county after closing.

**DOM (Days on Market)** — How many days a listing has been active. A market-velocity indicator.

**Disclosure** — A seller's signed statement about known property defects. In Texas, the OP-H (TREC) or TAR 1406 form.

**Dual agency** — When one agent or brokerage represents both buyer and seller in the same transaction. Has specific disclosure requirements. Treated as a high-risk situation requiring broker review.

## E

**Effective date** — The date the last party signs the contract AND the other party (or their broker) receives notice. Day 1 of all contract deadlines starts the day after the effective date.

**EMD (Earnest Money Deposit)** — The buyer's good-faith deposit, delivered to title or escrow within 3 days of effective date. Typical in Austin: 1% of sale price. Forfeited if the buyer defaults; recovered if termination is within an allowed window.

**Escrow** — A neutral third party (usually title) holding funds and documents until closing.

**Estate sale** — A sale of property owned by an estate (deceased owner). Usually requires probate court involvement. Treated as an edge case requiring broker review.

**Executed contract** — A contract signed by all parties with notice received. The source of truth for all transaction deadlines.

## F

**Fair Housing** — Federal law prohibiting discrimination in real estate transactions based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, or disability. The Agency's compliance scan defaults to fair-housing protection.

**FHA loan** — A government-backed mortgage. Has specific property condition requirements that can impact older homes.

## H

**HOA (Homeowners Association)** — A neighborhood or community association that enforces deed restrictions and collects dues. In Texas, requires the TREC 11-7 addendum and an HOA estoppel (resale certificate) from the HOA before closing.

**HOA estoppel** — A certificate from the HOA listing dues, special assessments, and known violations. Takes 7-10 business days; fees may apply.

## I

**IABS (Information About Brokerage Services)** `(TX)` — A required TREC form explaining how Texas real estate agency relationships work. Must be provided at first substantive communication with a buyer or seller.

**Inspection** — A licensed inspector's evaluation of the property. Buyer pays. Typically scheduled during the option period. Inspector identifies issues; severity assessment requires a specialist (structural engineer, HVAC contractor, etc.) for any item flagged.

**Inspection of repairs** — A follow-up inspection 5-7 days before closing to verify any agreed-on repairs were completed. Buyer's responsibility to schedule.

## L

**LE (Loan Estimate)** — A standardized document the lender provides within 3 business days of loan application. Itemizes estimated loan terms and costs. Compare against the CD before closing.

**Lead-based paint disclosure** — A federally required disclosure for homes built before 1978. Both parties sign.

**Listing agent** — The real estate agent representing the seller.

**Listing agreement** — The contract between a seller and a listing agent. Authorizes the agent to market and sell the property.

## M

**MLS (Multiple Listing Service)** — A database of active and recently sold property listings, accessible only to licensed real estate professionals. In Texas, the primary MLS for Austin is ACTRIS.

## N

**NAR (National Association of Realtors)** — The largest trade association for real estate agents in the U.S. A 2024 class-action settlement (Sitzer/Burnett) changed how buyer's agents are paid and required signed buyer representation agreements before showings.

## O

**Option fee** — A non-refundable fee the buyer pays the seller for the right to terminate the contract during the option period. Typical in Austin: $100-500.

**Option period** — A contracted window (often 5-10 calendar days in Texas) during which the buyer can terminate the contract for any reason and recover earnest money. The clock starts day after effective date.

## P

**POA (Power of Attorney)** — A legal document allowing one party to sign on behalf of another. Required if a buyer or seller can't attend closing. Must be approved by title and lender in advance.

## R

**Rep agreement** — Shorthand for buyer representation agreement or listing agreement. See those entries.

**RESO Web API** — The standardized API for MLS data, accessible to brokerages through providers like Bridge Interactive. Not used by The Agency v1 (paste-based design).

**REO (Real Estate Owned)** — A bank-owned property after foreclosure. Treated as an edge case requiring broker review.

## S

**Settlement statement** — The line-item summary of all financial transactions at closing. Reviewed by both parties before signing.

**Short sale** — A sale where the seller owes more on the mortgage than the property is worth, and the lender agrees to accept less. Lender-specific timelines override standard. Edge case requiring broker review.

**Sitzer/Burnett** — The 2023 class-action lawsuit against NAR and major brokerages that led to the 2024 settlement requiring written buyer representation agreements before showings.

**SPS (Seller's Property Disclosure)** — Same as Seller's Disclosure (TREC OP-H or TAR 1406 in Texas).

## T

**T-47 affidavit** `(TX)` — A signed seller affidavit that allows the buyer and title company to use the seller's existing property survey instead of paying for a new one.

**TAR (Texas Association of Realtors)** `(TX)` — The Texas state trade association. Promulgates many of the addenda used alongside TREC forms.

**Title** — The legal ownership of a property. Title company researches the property's history for any liens, defects, or claims before closing.

**Title commitment** — A title company's preliminary report on title status. Usually delivered within 20 days of effective date. Buyer can object to defects.

**TILA-RESPA** — Federal law requiring the 3-business-day Closing Disclosure rule and other consumer protections in real estate.

**TREC (Texas Real Estate Commission)** `(TX)` — The Texas state regulator for real estate. Promulgates standard contract forms that Texas license holders must use.

**TREC 20-17** `(TX)` — The standard Texas residential resale contract (current version; the number updates).

**TREC 39** `(TX)` — The Texas amendment form, used to modify an executed contract (e.g., for inspection renegotiation).

**TXR-1501** `(TX)` — The TAR Residential Buyer/Tenant Representation Agreement form. Common in Texas.

## V

**VA loan** — A mortgage backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. Available to qualifying veterans. Has specific property condition requirements that can complicate older homes.

## W

**Walk-through (final)** — The buyer's pre-closing visit to verify the property is in agreed condition. Typically 24-48 hours before closing.

**Wire fraud** — The #1 scam in real estate. Scammers impersonate the title company at the last minute and email "updated" wire instructions, redirecting the buyer's down payment to themselves. The Agency's compliance scan flags any wire-related request and the system never relays wire instructions.
