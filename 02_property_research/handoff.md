# Handoff Protocol

## What I Receive

- Property address.
- Listing packet or MLS sheet.
- Client criteria.
- Research question.
- Time sensitivity.
- Any source links approved by the team.

## What I Produce

- Research brief.
- Source list with date checked.
- Facts, signals, questions, and recommendations.
- Client-ready takeaway draft.
- Handoff packet to client communication or transaction coordination.

## Required Packet Fields

Every handoff must preserve:

- `Matter ID`
- `Stage`
- `Blocking` missing information
- `Helpful` missing information
- `Source of truth`
- `Source checked at`
- `Needs recheck before`
- `Reviewer needed`
- `Review reason`
- `Cannot proceed until`
- `Human Review` gate

## Handoff To Client Communication

Use when the research needs to be turned into a client email, text, call recap, or showing recommendation.

Include:

- Matter ID.
- One-paragraph plain-English takeaway.
- Facts safe to share.
- Items that need caveats.
- Items Diana must verify first.
- Any phrasing constraints, especially fair-housing-sensitive wording.

## Handoff To Transaction Coordinator

Use when research creates deal tasks.

Include:

- Matter ID.
- Property address.
- Contract or representation status.
- Inspection, permit, title, HOA, survey, or disclosure questions.
- Deadline if known.
- Who should own each follow-up.

## Return To Orchestrator When

- Source access is blocked.
- The request requires an attorney, inspector, lender, appraiser, title company, or broker decision.
- A client asks for a conclusion that would create fair-housing or professional-advice risk.
