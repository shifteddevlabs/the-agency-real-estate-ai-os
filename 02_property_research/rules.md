# Rules

## Research Stack

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Team-provided MLS sheet, seller disclosure, listing packet, or brokerage-approved data.
2. County appraisal district and public records.
3. City of Austin open data, permits, code, and GIS records.
4. Census, HUD, FHFA, Realtor.com Research, Redfin Data Center, and other public aggregate datasets.
5. Licensed data provider such as ATTOM if available.
6. General web search only for background context, never as the only source for a material claim.

## Source Discipline

Every output must include:

- Source name.
- Date checked.
- What the source can and cannot prove.
- Any data-quality warning.

Use labels:

- `Fact`: directly supported by source.
- `Signal`: supported but not conclusive.
- `Question`: needs human or professional follow-up.
- `Recommendation`: Diana/team judgment needed before using.

## Research Confidence

Every research brief gets one confidence label:

| Confidence | Meaning |
|---|---|
| High | Primary source checked recently, limits known, no conflicts |
| Medium | Reliable source, but stale, partial, or missing one verification |
| Low | Background-only, conflicting, volatile, or not client-ready |

Every material claim also gets one usability label:

- `Can share with client`
- `Internal only`
- `Needs Diana verification`
- `Needs professional review`
- `Do not use yet`

## Fair Housing And Steering Rules

- Do not rank neighborhoods for clients based on protected-class assumptions.
- Do not summarize demographics as suitability.
- Do not answer "is it safe" with a conclusion. Provide objective public data sources and suggest the client review them.
- Schools may be discussed only as objective district or boundary information, with a verification warning.

## Daily Operating Brief Signals To Produce

When updating research, name:

- Research confidence.
- Source checked date.
- Needs recheck before.
- Source conflicts.
- Client-shareable versus internal-only status.
- Next owner.
- Next check date.

## Never

- Never say "this is a good investment" without assumptions and human review.
- Never estimate repair costs unless sourced from a professional quote.
- Never treat a Zestimate, AVM, or list price as market value.
- Never reuse listing photos or content without permission.
- Never produce client-ready advice without noting what should be verified.
