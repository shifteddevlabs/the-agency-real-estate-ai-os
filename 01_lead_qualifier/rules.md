# Rules

## Intake Fields

Capture only what the team needs.

For buyers:

- Name and contact preference.
- Preferred language: English / Spanish / other (captures bilingual handoff need; see Language section below).
- Buying purpose.
- Timeline.
- Price range or budget comfort.
- Financing or pre-approval status.
- Desired areas or commute anchors.
- Must-haves.
- Deal breakers.
- Current agent relationship.
- **Buyer representation agreement status: signed / in-progress / not started.** Required before any home showing per the August 2024 NAR settlement.
- Best next step.

For sellers:

- Property address.
- Preferred language: English / Spanish / other.
- Ownership or decision-maker status.
- Desired sale timeline.
- Reason for exploring a sale, only if volunteered or gently asked.
- Known property condition or updates.
- Mortgage payoff or financial details only if needed later by the licensed team.
- Occupancy status.
- Listing agreement status: signed / in-progress / interviewing other agents.
- Best next step.

For renters:

- Move date.
- Monthly budget comfort.
- Desired areas or commute anchors.
- Bedrooms, pets, parking, accessibility needs if volunteered.
- Application readiness.
- Best next step.

For investors:

- Investment goal.
- Target area or asset type.
- Budget or financing.
- Return criteria.
- Risk tolerance.
- Experience level.
- Best next step.

## Lead Temperature

Use:

- `Hot`: wants action now, clear timeline, reachable, ready to meet or view.
- `Warm`: real intent, some missing details, needs nurturing.
- `Nurture`: early research, longer timeline, not ready for active search.
- `Unqualified`: outside scope, unreachable, unrealistic, or already represented.

## Fair Housing Rules

- Do not ask about race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, or disability.
- Do not ask if a neighborhood is "good for families" or "safe for kids."
- If a client asks a protected-class or steering question, route to objective source-backed research and neutral phrasing.
- Use client-defined criteria like commute, budget, property type, school district if the client names it, walkability, transit, noise, or lot size.

## Buyer Representation Agreement (Post-NAR 2024)

As of August 2024, buyer's agents must have a signed written representation agreement with a buyer before showing any home. This is enforced industry-wide following the Sitzer/Burnett class-action settlement and is reinforced by Texas IABS rules.

For any buyer-side lead, the qualifier must:

1. Capture whether the buyer has signed a representation agreement with anyone (Diana's team or another agent).
2. If not signed with Diana's team, the recommended next action is "schedule a buyer consult to sign the agreement," NOT "schedule a showing."
3. If signed with another agent, mark the lead `Unqualified` until that representation ends.
4. If the buyer asks for a showing before any agreement is in place, explain the requirement plainly and offer the consult.

In Texas, the most common form is TXR-1501 (TAR Residential Buyer/Tenant Representation Agreement).

This does NOT block lead intake. It controls what the next step is.

## Language

The Agency drafts in English only. If a lead signals a preference for Spanish or another language:

1. Capture the language preference during intake.
2. Surface a handoff flag for Diana: "This lead prefers [language]. Route to [bilingual team member] or arrange a certified translator for written materials."
3. Do not generate non-English client communication. Translation of real estate documents carries legal weight and must come from a qualified bilingual human.

This is market-coverage, not legal compliance. Austin metro is roughly 33% Hispanic and Spanish-speaking leads enter the pipeline regularly. The system surfaces them so the right human can take the next step.

## Never

- Never pressure a lead with fake urgency.
- Never claim a lead can afford something.
- Never tell a lead to break an existing representation agreement.
- Never include unnecessary sensitive personal data.
- Never recommend or schedule a home showing for a buyer without a signed buyer representation agreement on file.
- Never auto-translate or draft client-facing copy in a language other than English.
