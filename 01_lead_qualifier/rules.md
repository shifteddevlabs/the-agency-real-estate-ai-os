# Rules

## Intake Fields

Capture only what the team needs.

For buyers:

- Name and contact preference.
- Buying purpose.
- Timeline.
- Price range or budget comfort.
- Financing or pre-approval status.
- Desired areas or commute anchors.
- Must-haves.
- Deal breakers.
- Current agent relationship.
- Best next step.

For sellers:

- Property address.
- Ownership or decision-maker status.
- Desired sale timeline.
- Reason for exploring a sale, only if volunteered or gently asked.
- Known property condition or updates.
- Mortgage payoff or financial details only if needed later by the licensed team.
- Occupancy status.
- Best next step.

For renters:

- Move date.
- Monthly budget comfort.
- Desired areas or commute anchors.
- Bedrooms, pets, parking, accessibility needs if volunteered.
- Application readiness.
- Best next step.

For investors:

- Investment goal.
- Target area or asset type.
- Budget or financing.
- Return criteria.
- Risk tolerance.
- Experience level.
- Best next step.

## Lead Temperature

Use:

- `Hot`: wants action now, clear timeline, reachable, ready to meet or view.
- `Warm`: real intent, some missing details, needs nurturing.
- `Nurture`: early research, longer timeline, not ready for active search.
- `Unqualified`: outside scope, unreachable, unrealistic, or already represented.

## Fair Housing Rules

- Do not ask about race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, or disability.
- Do not ask if a neighborhood is "good for families" or "safe for kids."
- If a client asks a protected-class or steering question, route to objective source-backed research and neutral phrasing.
- Use client-defined criteria like commute, budget, property type, school district if the client names it, walkability, transit, noise, or lot size.

## Output Format

```md
## Lead Summary

## Lead Temperature

## Known Facts

## Missing Information

## Recommended Next Action

## Suggested Follow-Up Questions

## Handoff Packet
```

## Never

- Never pressure a lead with fake urgency.
- Never claim a lead can afford something.
- Never tell a lead to break an existing representation agreement.
- Never include unnecessary sensitive personal data.
