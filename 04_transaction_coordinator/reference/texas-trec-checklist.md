# Texas TREC Transaction Checklist

A working checklist for Texas residential transactions using TREC promulgated forms. This is a teaching reference, not legal advice. Diana, the broker, the title company, the lender, and the attorney are still the source of truth on any specific deal.

## What This Reference Is

A flat list of the documents, dates, and decisions that show up in nearly every Texas residential transaction. Use it to:

- Build a deal checklist when a contract is executed.
- Spot what is missing when an update comes in mid-deal.
- Decide which escalation owner (broker, title, lender, attorney, inspector) owns a question.

## Standard TREC Forms (most common)

TREC promulgated forms must be used by Texas license holders for the transactions they cover. Most residential resale deals run on:

| Form | Purpose | Who fills it |
|---|---|---|
| TREC 20-17 (or current version) | One to Four Family Residential Contract (Resale) | Buyer's agent drafts, both parties sign |
| TREC 23-19 | Third Party Financing Addendum | If buyer is financing |
| TREC 11-7 | Addendum for Property Subject to Mandatory Membership in a POA | If property is in an HOA |
| TREC 40-10 | Third Party Financing Condition Addendum (older alt) | Some lenders |
| TREC OP-H | Seller's Disclosure Notice | Seller, unless exempt |
| TREC 38-7 | Notice of Buyer's Termination of Contract | Buyer terminating during option |
| TREC 36-9 | Release of Earnest Money | When closing or terminating |
| TAR 1406 | Seller's Disclosure (TAR version) | Common alternative to OP-H |
| T-47 Affidavit | Allows seller's existing survey to be used | Seller signs at closing or earlier |
| Lead-Based Paint Addendum | Required for homes built before 1978 | Federal requirement, both parties |
| TXR 1501 (TAR Buyer Rep) | Buyer's Representation Agreement | Required before any showing (NAR 2024); buyer and buyer's agent |
| TREC IABS | Information About Brokerage Services | Required at first substantive communication |

Confirm the current form versions with the broker or TREC.gov before any reliance.

## Buyer Representation Agreement (Required Since August 2024)

Following the NAR class-action settlement (Sitzer/Burnett), buyer's agents must have a signed written representation agreement with a buyer before showing any home. This is a hard prerequisite for buyer-side work.

**For transaction coordination this means:**

- A signed buyer rep agreement (TXR-1501 or equivalent) must exist in the deal file BEFORE any showing or offer is processed.
- The agreement specifies how the buyer's agent gets paid (seller pays, buyer pays, or split). This number must match the financing application and the closing documents. Discrepancies trigger an amendment or renegotiation.
- If the agreement was signed before the August 2024 rule change, check whether re-execution under current rules is needed. Escalate to broker if unclear.
- Listing-side: verify the buyer's side has a signed agreement before engaging in tour scheduling or offer review. (Their compliance, not yours, but worth confirming.)

**Common failure modes:**

- Agent shows the home first, signs the agreement later. Industry compliance violation. Broker may discipline.
- Compensation in the rep agreement does not match what the seller offered. Resolve before contract or buyer brings the gap in cash.
- Verbal agreement only. No protection for the agent or the buyer; not enforceable under current rules.

## Days Calculation (this trips people up)

- **Texas residential contracts count CALENDAR days,** not business days, unless the specific paragraph says otherwise.
- **Day 1** is the day AFTER the effective date (date of last signature with notice).
- A deadline that falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or federal holiday rolls to the next business day. (Paragraph 22 of the standard contract.)
- The option period is in calendar days but the buyer must deliver the termination notice and option fee receipt by 5 PM local time on the last day.

If an update says "option ends Friday," verify against the executed contract's effective date. Do not trust the date someone typed in an email.

## The Effective Date

The contract's effective date is the date the last party signed AND the other party (or their broker) received notice of that signing. If the effective date is unclear:

- Mark every downstream deadline as `unverified`.
- Escalate to broker or title to confirm.
- Never give the client a deadline based on assumed effective date.

## Earnest Money

- Typical: 1% of sale price in Austin metro, but varies.
- Delivered to title or escrow within 3 days of effective date (per paragraph 5 of the standard contract). The 3-day window itself rolls forward on weekends.
- Late delivery is a default. Do not assume it was delivered just because the email says so. Confirm with title.
- Wire fraud risk: never share wiring instructions over email. Title company calls back to verify, no exceptions.

## Option Period

- Buyer pays a non-refundable option fee (often $100 to $500 in Austin).
- During the option period, the buyer can terminate for any reason and recover earnest money.
- Length is negotiated. Typical: 5 to 10 calendar days.
- Inspection happens here. Renegotiation requests should be drafted before option ends.
- The buyer must deliver termination notice AND have the option fee actually received by 5 PM on the last day. Both conditions matter.

See `option-period-playbook.md` for the full sequence.

## Inspection

- Buyer schedules. Buyer pays.
- Inspector is licensed by TREC. Inspection report typically arrives 1 to 3 days after the inspection.
- If findings raise concerns, the buyer can:
  1. Terminate during option (cleanest exit).
  2. Request repairs (amendment to the contract).
  3. Request a credit at closing (price reduction or contribution to closing costs).
  4. Accept as-is and move forward.
- Repair requests are negotiated through an Amendment (TREC 39 or current version), signed by both parties.

See `inspection-renegotiation-playbook.md` for the negotiation sequence.

## Appraisal (financed deals)

- Lender orders the appraisal after option ends and financing application is complete.
- Appraised value below contract price triggers the Third Party Financing Addendum's appraisal provision: buyer can terminate, renegotiate, or bring cash to cover the gap.
- Appraisal contingency timeline is dictated by the financing addendum, not the main contract.

## Title

- Title commitment is typically delivered within 20 days of effective date.
- Buyer has a window (often 5 days) to object to title defects. Objections trigger a cure period.
- Survey: T-47 affidavit allows seller's existing survey if the buyer and title agree. Otherwise the buyer pays for a new survey.
- HOA estoppel (the resale certificate from the HOA) takes 7 to 10 business days and the HOA may charge a fee. Order early.

## Closing Week

See `closing-week-checklist.md` for the full sequence.

Hot items:

- Final walk-through (24 to 48 hours before closing).
- Closing Disclosure (CD) issued by lender at least 3 business days before closing.
- Wire instructions verified by phone with title, never by email.
- Photo ID matching the contract name (no nicknames).
- Settlement statement reviewed by Diana or assigned agent.

## What This Reference Does NOT Cover

- New construction (different contracts, builder addenda, separate disclosure rules).
- Farm and ranch transactions.
- Commercial transactions.
- Probate, divorce, or court-ordered sales.
- Investor transactions with assignment clauses.
- Cash deals (skip the financing and appraisal sections; everything else still applies).

Escalate any of the above to the broker.

## Mandatory Escalations

Always pull the broker, attorney, or appropriate specialist in when:

- A wire instruction request comes from anyone other than the title officer of record, by phone.
- A party requests a contract modification mid-deal beyond a standard amendment.
- A dispute arises between buyer and seller agents.
- A fair-housing or discrimination concern surfaces.
- A material change in the property's condition occurs (e.g., damage between contract and closing).
- A default notice is served or considered.

## Source Of Truth

The executed contract is the source of truth. If a deadline, name, or term differs between an email and the contract, the contract wins. Always check.
