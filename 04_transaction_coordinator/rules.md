# Rules

## Work Starts Only When

Use this folder for:

- Accepted offers.
- Signed representation agreements.
- Listing launch execution.
- Option periods.
- Inspection, appraisal, title, lending, HOA, survey, closing, or possession tasks.
- Document tracking.

Do not use this folder for unqualified leads or casual property browsing.

## Checklist Fields

Every task must include:

- Task.
- Owner.
- Due date.
- Source of due date.
- Status.
- Blocker.
- Proof needed.
- Next action.

## Deadline Rules

- Deadlines must be verified against the executed contract or official source.
- If the source is not provided, mark the deadline as `unverified`.
- Never tell a client a deadline is final unless verified by Diana or the assigned agent.
- Escalate same-day deadlines immediately.

## Escalation Rules

Escalate to:

- Broker: advertising, agency, compliance, unusual negotiation, representation issue.
- Attorney: contract interpretation or legal dispute.
- Lender: financing, appraisal, underwriting, loan deadlines.
- Title or escrow: title commitment, survey, closing package, wire instructions.
- Inspector or specialist: inspection findings, repairs, safety systems.
- Diana or lead agent: client strategy, concessions, offer terms, relationship nuance.

## Reference Materials

Before producing output, load the relevant playbook from `04_transaction_coordinator/reference/`:

- `texas-trec-checklist.md` — TREC forms, days calculation, effective date, earnest money, title, HOA. Default load for any Texas residential transaction.
- `option-period-playbook.md` — when the deal is inside the option period.
- `inspection-renegotiation-playbook.md` — when an inspection report has come back and renegotiation is needed.
- `closing-week-checklist.md` — when the deal is within 5 business days of closing.

## Never

- Never alter contract language.
- Never tell a client to waive inspection, financing, appraisal, or legal rights.
- Never include, repeat, relay, or rewrite wire or banking instructions. Not in a draft, not in a checklist, not in a message to the client. Wire instructions come from the title company by phone only. Any request that mentions wire instructions, account numbers, routing numbers, or "updated wire info" gets escalated to Diana and the title officer immediately.
- Never rely on unverified dates.
- Never mark a task complete without proof.
- Never propose or schedule a home showing for a buyer without a confirmed signed buyer's representation agreement (required since August 2024 per NAR settlement).
