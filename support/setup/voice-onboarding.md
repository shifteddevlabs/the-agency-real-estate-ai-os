# Voice Setup

Use this once before The Agency writes real client-facing drafts for Diana's team.

The goal is not to copy Diana word for word. The goal is to make drafts sound like something her team would actually send: clear, calm, useful, and not generic.

Use redacted or mock examples if this folder will be public.

## Collect These Examples

Ask Diana for:

- 4 real emails she likes, with private details removed.
- 4 short texts or follow-ups she would send.
- 1 buyer, seller, listing, or inspection update that went well.
- 1 draft she dislikes, with notes on why.
- Her usual greeting and sign-off.
- Words she uses often.
- Words she never wants in client messages.

## Ask Diana

Ask these in writing. Natural answers are better than polished answers.

1. A buyer loves a home, but you see inspection or condition concerns. How do you slow them down without killing the excitement?
2. A seller wants to price above the comps. How do you explain the risk?
3. A lead says they are "just browsing." How do you reply without sounding pushy?
4. A client asks whether a neighborhood is safe or good for families. How do you redirect that safely?
5. A buyer is nervous about making an offer. What do you say?
6. An inspection report has several issues. How do you frame the next step?
7. A deal is waiting on lender, title, or another party. How do you update the client without blaming anyone?
8. A client has not replied in a few days. How do you follow up?
9. What sounds too salesy to you?
10. What should clients feel after reading a message from your team?

## Quick Scenario Prompts

Use these if Diana does not have enough old examples ready.

```text
Pretend I am a nervous first-time buyer. The inspection report looks long and I am worried I made a mistake. Reply the way you would naturally text me.
```

```text
Pretend I am a seller who wants to list 8% above the comps because my house feels nicer. Explain your advice in your normal voice.
```

```text
Pretend I am a buyer lead who filled out a form but gave almost no details. Write your first reply.
```

```text
Pretend I asked whether a neighborhood is safe and good for families. Give me the safe version of how you would answer.
```

## Check A Draft

Score test drafts from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Sounds like Diana, accurate, safe, and useful |
| 4 | Usable with small edits |
| 3 | Correct but generic |
| 2 | Needs major voice edits |
| 1 | Unsafe, inaccurate, or unusable |

Client-facing drafts should score 4 or 5 before Diana sends them.

## Privacy Rule

Do not put real client names, exact addresses, budgets, financing details, inspection findings, or personal circumstances in a public repo. Use redacted or mock details.
